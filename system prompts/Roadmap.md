# Roadmap

## Roadmap

This document tells you what you have, what order to use it in, and how to actually run it. Start here, then open Phase 1.

---

## What's in the Package

**Eleven tools, organized into four setup phases plus an ongoing toolkit.** Each tool is a markdown file containing a system prompt that you'll paste into any AI chatbot.

**Four documents you'll create during setup.** Your Named Goals, your Financial Snapshot, your Environment Audit, and your Active Primary Causes. The Snapshot is the central one. Most other tools either feed into it or pull from it.

**This roadmap.** A reference you can come back to anytime you need to find your place.

---

## The Four Setup Phases

Run the phases in order. Each one earns the next.

### Phase 1: Orientation

Name where you're going and map where you are.

- **Goal Definer** — names 3-4 specific financial goals
- **Financial Force Snapshot Generator** — produces your Financial Snapshot

Time: 60-75 minutes total.

### Phase 2: Diagnosis

Understand the forces operating on your spending, both external and internal.

- **Environment Audit** — maps external forces pushing you to spend
- **Primary Causes Diagnostic** — identifies internal spending patterns

Time: 60-75 minutes total.

### Phase 3: Design

Turn your snapshot, goals, and diagnoses into a working budget.

- **Budget Builder** — produces your personalized budget plus an implementation method

**Video walkthrough:** After you've built your budget, watch a real example of how one person set up and runs theirs day to day. The terminology is slightly different in places (it was recorded before this system existed), but the method itself is exactly what the Budget Builder covers. [Watch the walkthrough](https://youtu.be/xOSOxmQqLPA?si=xzGfvWA9OsGWFsxn)

Time: 20-30 minutes.

### Phase 4: Execution

Bridge the plan on paper to the plan in real life.

- **Launch Plan Generator** — produces your 30-day setup-and-run plan

Time: 15-20 minutes.

## The Ongoing Toolkit

After setup, these tools become your ongoing practice. Use them when the situation calls for them.

- **Spending Regret Decoder** — diagnose a purchase you regret. Surfaces what was happening externally and internally when you made the decision, names the pattern, suggests a fix.
- **Monthly Financial Force Review** — run once a month. Produces an updated snapshot from your latest numbers and goal progress.
- **Money Finder** — use when your financial force is negative, stuck, or just lower than you want it. Walks through your actual numbers hunting for money, easy wins first, and produces a Force Plan of specific moves with real monthly dollar values.
- **Plan Recalibrator** — use when something disrupts the plan (job change, surprise expense, life event). Rebuilds the system from the new reality.
- **Goal Progress Checker** — periodic check-in on whether you're on track for your named goals. Surfaces gaps and helps you decide whether to adjust the goal or the plan.

## How to Run a Tool

1. **Open a fresh chat** in Claude, ChatGPT, Gemini, or whatever AI chatbot you use. Each tool runs in its own conversation. Don't chain multiple tools in one chat.
2. **Open the tool's markdown file** and copy everything from the first divider onward. Each file has instructions at the top.
3. **Paste it into the chat.** The AI reads the prompt and starts the conversation.
4. **Have your documents ready.** If the tool asks for your snapshot, goals, or other outputs, you'll paste them in when prompted. Keep them somewhere accessible.
5. **Save the outputs.** Every tool that generates a document produces it as markdown you can copy. Keep them in one folder or note so you can find them later.
6. **Consider using voice input.** Most AI chatbots have a voice option where you can speak your answers and it transcribes them. For the reflective tools especially (Goal Definer, Primary Causes Diagnostic), speaking tends to produce much better results than typing. When people type, they give the short version. When they speak, they say more, which gives the AI more to work with.
7. **A tip if you use Claude.** Claude's free plan includes Projects, which work like folders that hold documents your chats can see. Create one called My Financial Folder, add your saved documents to it as you produce them (goals, snapshot, audit, causes, budget), and run each tool as a new chat inside that Project. The AI can then pull from your documents without you pasting them in every time. Any chatbot works fine with plain pasting. This just saves a step if Claude is your pick.

## A Few Things Worth Knowing About AI

**The AI is not a financial advisor.** It's a thinking tool. It helps you organize your numbers, see your patterns, and build a plan that fits your life. It's done a lot of research and it's pretty good at this. But it doesn't know everything, and it can occasionally get something wrong. If a number doesn't look right or a suggestion doesn't make sense for your situation, trust your own judgment. You know your life better than any AI does.

**Every conversation will be slightly different.** AI doesn't give identical answers every time. That's actually a good thing. It means the conversation adapts to what you tell it, how you phrase things, and what details you share. But it also means if you run the same tool twice, the output might be worded differently or organized a little differently. The substance should be consistent. The exact wording won't be.

**You are the decision-maker.** The AI will suggest, recommend, and push back when something seems off. That's what it's designed to do. But every number in your budget, every goal you name, every decision you make is yours. You own the output. The AI is the guide. You're the one walking the path.

## Before You Start

**Block out the time.** Setup is roughly four hours total. You can do it in one sitting or split it across phases. What doesn't work is squeezing it into 15-minute gaps. The diagnostic tools especially need real attention.

**Have your bank and credit card statements available.** The Snapshot Generator works best with real numbers from the last 3 months. You can upload CSVs, share screenshots of your transaction history, or estimate manually. Real data is better, but manual estimation works.

**Keep account numbers out of it.** None of the tools ever need account numbers, logins, or your Social Security number. Amounts, merchant names, and dates are all the work takes. If a statement or screenshot shows an account number, crop it or cover it before sharing.

**No prerequisite knowledge required.** If you've never budgeted before, that's fine. The tools push back when numbers seem off, teach you what you need to know, and produce something realistic for your situation.

**Partnered? Decide together or solo.** Some couples go through setup together. Others have one partner do setup and bring the other in afterward. Both work. If you go solo first, you can revisit any tool together once you've shared what you produced.

**Expect to adjust.** Your first snapshot won't be perfect. Your first budget will probably need tweaking. Goals can shift. The Monthly Review and Plan Recalibrator tools handle drift and surprises. You're picking a direction to start moving, not signing anything permanent.
